Section 9
Responsible AI — Ethics & Risk Framework
Four dimensions of responsible AI deployment for rural NBFC credit scoring. AI output was solid but generic — East/Central India-specific context (tribal communities, seasonal labour, informal economy) was added through managerial judgment.
4
Ethics dimensions
⚖️
Demographic Bias in Credit Scoring
Proxy variable bias risk: mobile data usage patterns can encode caste/gender biases. Fairness audits required. Special attention to women borrowers and lower-income communities in East/Central India's tribal areas and informal economy segments.
🔐
Data Privacy — Account Aggregator
Consent challenges for digitally illiterate rural borrowers under the AA framework. BC-mediated local language consent (own addition — AI did not flag this). Strict data minimization and purpose limitation required.
🤖
AI Hallucination Safeguards
AI credit scores must never be sole basis for lending decisions. Human-in-the-loop decisioning as formal policy requirement (Section 9.4). All AI-generated statistics verified against original sources before inclusion in report.
🌾
Rural Context Specificity
Seasonal income volatility in agri lending not captured by standard AI models. Informal economy participants, seasonal labour, and non-traditional creditworthiness signals require human override capability in all scoring decisions.
